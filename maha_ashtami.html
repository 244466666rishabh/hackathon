Maha Ashtami is the most powerful day of Durga Puja, marking the peak of devotion and divine energy. It commemorates the moment when Goddess Durga, as Devi Chamunda, defeated the demons Chaman and Munda symbolizing the victory of righteousness over evil.

Sandhi Puja: The most sacred ritual, performed at the junction of Ashtami and Navami, honors the exact moment when the Goddess destroyed Mahishasura. 108 lamps and 108 lotus flowers are offered.

Kumari Puja: A young girl is worshipped as a living embodiment of the Goddess. She is adorned with flowers, sweets, and gifts in reverence to divine femininity.

Pushpanjali: Devotees offer flowers and prayers seeking courage, purity, and strength.
